CHALLENGE RULES – COMMON QUESTIONS
=====================================

Q: Do I need to run on a specific date?
A: No! You can complete your challenge on any day within the challenge window. No fixed date is required.

Q: Is there a cut-off time or minimum pace I need to maintain?
A: No. There is no cut-off time and no minimum pace. All NextMile challenges are completely self-paced.

Q: Can I walk instead of run?
A: For running challenges (Momentum Run, Women's Run, Miles for Mom), you should run or jog. For the 10K Steps Challenge, walking counts! For the Conquest Ride, cycling is required. For the 100KM in 30 Days Challenge, you can walk, jog, run, or cycle — any mix counts toward your 100 KM total.

Q: Can I participate from outside India?
A: Our challenges are virtual, so you can complete the activity from anywhere. However, medal shipping is currently available within India only.

Q: I missed the challenge window. Can I still submit?
A: Your challenge window is 30 days from your personal registration date — so check your registration date to see if you're still within the window. This applies to all events: Miles for Mom, Momentum Run, Women's Run, 10K Steps, Conquest Ride Cycling, and the 100KM in 30 Days Challenge. If your 30-day window has passed, please contact us at support@gonextmile.in.

Q: Can I do more than the required distance?
A: Yes! You can always exceed the required distance. Just make sure your screenshot shows at least the minimum required distance.

Q: How does the 100KM Challenge work? Do I have to run 100 KM in one go?
A: No — the 100KM in 30 Days Challenge is cumulative, not a single-session event. Track your activity day by day with any fitness app, mixing running, jogging, walking, or cycling however you like. Only once your running total reaches the full 100 KM do you submit your screenshot(s) — in ONE final submission, not day by day.

Q: Do I need a GPS tracker or smartwatch?
A: No. Your phone's built-in fitness app or any free fitness tracking app is sufficient.

Q: Is there a minimum age to participate? Can children/minors join?
A: There is no published minimum age policy right now. Do not confirm or guess eligibility for minors — direct the customer to contact support@gonextmile.in or WhatsApp +91 95352 12425 to confirm before registering a minor. Never infer eligibility from the Family/Group registration options.

CONQUEST RIDE 25KM - PREP GUIDE
=================================
Download: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1aPqnO1ff5oL91wQDZovc_1bRQST4Xl00/view?usp=sharing

CHAPTER 1 - WHY MOVEMENT MATTERS
When you ride, your body comes alive. Your breath settles. Your mind clears. You move with rhythm, not resistance.
Cycling builds endurance, strengthens your legs, and helps you stay steady over distance.
Quote: "The body achieves what the mind believes." - Sachin Tendulkar

Common mistakes riders face:
Starting too fast and tiring early, overthinking the distance, riding without a plan, losing rhythm midway, letting doubt take over.

How to stay on track:
Start easy. Find your rhythm. Ride at a pace you can sustain. Break the distance into smaller stretches. Stay relaxed. Keep moving forward.

CHAPTER 2 - PREP FRAMEWORK (7-10 DAYS BEFORE YOUR RIDE)
Beginner Framework:
3 Easy Rides of 8-12 KM at relaxed pace, 1 Slightly Longer Ride of 15-18 KM, 1 Rest/Mobility Day.
Key: Ride at a pace where you can breathe comfortably. Comfort matters more than speed.

Intermediate Framework:
2 Easy Rides of 12-18 KM, 1 Longer Ride of 18-22 KM steady and controlled, 1 Recovery Day.
Key: Keep effort steady, not aggressive. Longer rides build confidence.

Planning tip: Break preparation into Rides (how often), Mindset (rule that keeps you going), Setup (route, timing, water, playlist).
If-Then rule: If I miss the morning, then I will ride in the evening.

CHAPTER 3 - MINDSET AND TECHNIQUE
Mindset cue: "Your mind will quit before your legs do. Don't let it."
Coach tip: When you feel overwhelmed, slow your pace and reset your breathing. A calm mind makes the ride feel easier.

3-Stage Mindset Flow:
Start: "Easy pace. Find your rhythm."
Middle: "Stay steady. One stretch at a time."
Finish: "You've got this. Finish strong."

Mindset tools: Break the distance into smaller parts, use simple cues like "steady", "smooth", "keep going", relax shoulders and grip, focus on slow steady breathing.

Technique:
Posture: Keep shoulders loose and grip light. Look ahead and avoid hunching. Better posture makes breathing easier and reduces fatigue.
Pedaling: Avoid pushing too hard. Focus on consistency over speed. Smooth pedaling conserves energy.
Breathing: Don't hold your breath. Slow down if breathing feels heavy. Your breath should guide your pace.

Warm-up (3-5 minutes before your ride):
Easy pedaling at slow pace, gentle leg movement or stretches, gradually build into your rhythm.

Pacing cues:
First stretch feels easy, middle stays steady, breathing guides your effort, save energy for the final stretch.

Finishing strong (last few KMs):
Sit a little taller, relax shoulders and grip, breathe deeper, keep pace controlled. A strong finish is not about speed, it is about staying in control till the end.

CHAPTER 4 - NUTRITION, HYDRATION AND RECOVERY

Hydration:
Before: 1 glass of water 20-30 mins before your ride.
During: Sip every 15-20 mins if ride is long.
After: Rehydrate with water or electrolytes.

Pre-ride food:
2-3 hours before: Banana and peanut butter, Idli/Poha/Upma, Oats, Toast and eggs.
30 minutes before: A few sips of water. Optional half banana.
Avoid: Oily or heavy food, too much dairy, trying anything new.

Recovery after your ride:
1. Cool Down: Ride slowly for 3-5 minutes at the end.
2. Stretch (hold each 20-30 seconds): Calves, Hamstrings, Quads, Glutes, Lower back.
3. Rehydrate and refuel within 30-45 mins: Banana and curd, peanut butter toast, smoothie, oats with nuts.
4. Sleep: 7-8 hours helps your body repair and restore energy.
5. Light movement on rest days: Ankle circles, gentle leg stretches, light movement.

CHAPTER 5 - KEEP THE MOMENTUM GOING
Build the identity, not just the habit. See yourself as someone who rides.
Even a short ride counts. Showing up matters more than doing it perfectly.
Make movement easy: Keep your cycle ready, choose simple routes, pick times that fit your day.

How to submit your ride:
1. Complete your chosen distance (10KM or 25KM) of cycling in a single day within 30 days of your registration.
2. Take a screenshot showing Distance, Time, and Date.
3. Submit at: https://gonextmile.in/pages/submit
4. Our team verifies and ships your medal within 7-10 days.
Accepted apps: Strava, Nike Run Club, Samsung Health, Apple Health, Mi Fitness.

10K STEPS CHALLENGE - PREP GUIDE
===================================

CHAPTER 1 - WHY MOVEMENT MATTERS
When you move, your body shifts into its most natural state. Running and walking release endorphins, improve circulation, enhance focus, and reduce anxiety.
Quote: "Small steps every day lead to big results." - Ratan Tata

Why consistency breaks:
All-or-nothing thinking (missing one day feels like failure), trying too much too soon, no routine anchor, high friction (gear not ready, timing unclear), doing it alone with no accountability.

How to stay consistent:
Start small (10-20 minutes is enough). Attach movement to a trigger (after morning tea, after shower, after work). Keep shoes visible and clothes ready. Fix a time (same window daily). Track tiny wins (a simple tick mark). Move with others for shared motivation.

CHAPTER 2 - BUILDING YOUR DAILY MOVEMENT RHYTHM

Framework 1 - Consistency Builder (for people starting fresh or on-and-off with movement):
Walk 15-20 minutes in the morning to set the habit for the day.
Walk 10-15 minutes later in the day (after lunch, between meetings, or evening).
On your 7 chosen days, extend one of these walks to reach 10,000 steps.
Key principles: Two short walks beat one skipped day. Duration matters less than repetition. Small days protect the habit.

Framework 2 - Consistency Stabiliser (for people who already walk regularly):
Morning walk: 20-30 minutes.
Midday walk: 15-20 minutes.
Evening walk: 15-25 minutes.
On 7 days, complete all three walks to comfortably cross 10,000 steps.
Key principles: Spread effort across the day. Never wait for one long window. Rhythm beats motivation.

CHAPTER 3 - MINDSET AND TECHNIQUE FOR EFFORTLESS WALKING
Mindset: "Your mind will quit before your legs do. Don't let it."
Coach tip: If you feel overwhelmed, slow down and reconnect with your breath. A calmer mind makes walking smoother.

3-Stage Flow:
Start: Relax shoulders. Easy pace.
Middle: One step at a time.
Finish: I showed up today.

Mindset tools: Count steps briefly to stay present, use a simple mantra like "Easy. Steady.", smile to release tension, focus on slow steady breathing.

Walking Technique:
Posture: Stand tall without stiffness, keep shoulders relaxed, look ahead.
Foot placement: Let steps land naturally under your body. Comfort matters more than form.
Breathing: Find a breathing rhythm you can sustain. If breathing becomes rushed, slow your pace.

Warm-up (before every walk):
Easy walking at relaxed pace, arm swings to loosen the shoulders, hip circles to reduce tightness, leg swings to wake up the legs.

Pacing cues:
First part feels relaxed and unhurried, middle stays steady and comfortable, breathing guides your effort, you finish without feeling drained.

Finishing your walk (last few minutes):
Stand tall without stiffness, relax shoulders and arms, let breathing slow and deepen naturally, gradually ease your pace.

CHAPTER 4 - NUTRITION, HYDRATION AND RECOVERY

Hydration:
Before walking: A glass of water 20-30 minutes before.
During the day: Sip water regularly, especially if you are moving often.
After longer walks: Water is usually enough. Electrolytes are optional.

Nutrition:
Carbohydrates support energy through the day. Protein helps your muscles recover. Fats provide steady energy. Fruits and vegetables support overall health. You do not need special food for walking. Regular, familiar meals are enough.
A few hours before walking: Simple meals like fruits, grains, eggs, or home-style food.
Avoid heavy, oily food or trying anything new. Walking feels best when your stomach feels calm.

Recovery:
1. Cool Down: A few minutes slowing your pace at the end.
2. Stretch (hold each 20-30 seconds): Calves, Hamstrings, Quads, Glutes, Hip flexors.
3. Rehydrate: Water or a light carb + protein snack (fruit, curd, toast, nuts).
4. Sleep: Aim for 7-8 hours when possible.
5. Light movement on rest days: Easy walking, gentle stretching, mobility.
Coach tip: If you feel stiff the day after a longer walk, spend 5 minutes on gentle mobility like ankle circles, leg swings, and hip openers.

CHAPTER 5 - KEEP THE MOMENTUM GOING
See yourself as someone who moves. Even a 10-minute walk keeps the identity alive.
It is better to show up small than wait for the perfect day.
When motivation dips, shrink the habit: do 5 minutes. A tiny win keeps the lifestyle alive.

CHAPTER 3 — MINDSET AND TECHNIQUE

A mindset that makes running feel easier.
A good run starts in your mind. When your thoughts are steady, your breath settles, your stride relaxes and the effort feels lighter. Mindset is the glue that holds your technique, pace, and confidence together.

CHAPTER 4 — NUTRITION, HYDRATION & RECOVERY

Daily nutrition and hydration:
Daily basics:
- Carbs = fuel
- Protein = repair
- Fats = sustained energy
- Hydration = performance
- Fruits & veggies = micronutrients

Hydration — Why It's Critical:
Even a 1% drop in hydration reduces performance. Small sips keep your heart, muscles, and cooling system efficient.
Before: 1 glass, 20–30 mins before
During: Sip every 15–20 mins (if run > 30 mins)
After: Water + electrolytes

Race Day Nutrition:
2–3 hours before: Banana + peanut butter, Idli / poha / upma, Oats, Toast + eggs

Avoid: Oily food, Heavy milk, New foods/supplements

Recovery — where real progress happens:
Your body improves after the workout — during recovery. Good recovery helps your muscles repair, reduces soreness, improves sleep, and keeps your training sustainable.

1. Cool Down — Ease Out of the Run: Spend 3–5 minutes slowing to a walk. This brings your heart rate down gradually and reduces post-run tightness.

2. Stretch the Big Muscles (hold each 20–30 seconds):
- Calves
- Hamstrings
- Quads
- Glutes
- Hip flexors

3. Rehydrate & Refuel: Water + electrolytes. A small carb + protein snack within 30–45 minutes. Examples: Banana + curd, peanut butter toast, smoothie, oats with nuts. This helps your muscles recover sooner and reduces fatigue the next day.

4. Sleep — The Most Underrated Recovery Tool: 7–8 hours of sleep improves muscle repair, hormones, and energy. Even one good night of sleep can make a next-day run feel dramatically easier.

5. Light Movement on Rest Days: Easy walking, stretching, or mobility keeps your body loose. Rest days aren't inactivity — they're gentle recovery days.

Coach Tip: If you feel stiff the day after a run, spend 5 minutes on mobility: ankle circles, leg swings, and hip openers. Little resets prevent small issues from becoming injuries.

Since the 100KM Challenge lets you mix running, jogging, walking, and cycling, this guide borrows directly from the prep guidance already covered above:
- For running/jogging/walking days: follow the warm-up, pacing, technique, nutrition, hydration, and recovery guidance from the WOMEN'S RUN / 10K STEPS sections above.
- For cycling days: follow the warm-up, pacing, technique, nutrition, hydration, and recovery guidance from the CONQUEST RIDE 25KM section above.
- Mix and match freely — the same recovery and consistency principles (small daily wins, fixed time windows, tracking tiny progress) apply regardless of which activity you choose on a given day.

Building toward 100KM:
The goal is cumulative distance, not a single hard effort. Spread it across your 30 days — for example, roughly 3-4 KM a day comfortably gets you there, but there's no required pace or daily minimum. Mix rest days, easy days, and longer days as needed.

How to submit your 100KM:
1. Track each session (run, walk, jog, or ride) with any fitness app as you go.
2. Once your cumulative total reaches at least 100 KM, take screenshot(s) clearly showing the cumulative distance and activity dates.
3. Submit at: https://tally.so/r/Gx54PO — ONE final submission only, after reaching the full 100 KM. Do not submit partial or day-by-day totals.
4. Our team verifies and ships your medal within 7-10 days.
Accepted apps: Strava, Google Fit, Apple Health, Nike Run Club.
